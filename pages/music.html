Bagpipes

Of all the instruments I have played, bagpipes are the closest to my heart. I began playing them when I was 12, but didn't become a fully realized piper until high school. There is a pretty strong community around playing this instrument, which participates in everything from intense band and solo competition to drunkenly playing for strangers on St. Patrick's day. This is part of what makes this instrument great.


Uilleann Pipes

Uilleann (il-an, which means "elbow" in Irish Gaelic) pipes are another instrument I love deeply. Where highland bagpipes have a somewhat rigid musical structure designed for playing with only other bagpipers (not to mention it's an instrument of war, with all of the military precision that comes along with that), uilleann pipes have a much "looser" style, that can take liberties with the music and play with a large variety of other instruments. The are much quieter and "mellower", often being played indoors. They are often used in movie soundtracks because they have a distinctly whimsical sound. Sometimes they'll even have a highland piper on screen with the sound of uilleann pipes coming out of the instrument (I'm looking at you, Braveheart).


Small Pipes

Small pipes are essentially a variant of highland pipes, but you guessed it, smaller. They're usually played sitting down and with the drones lying over the chest. They have the exact same fingering as highland pipes so many highland pipers buy a set for fun. They're great for playing inside since they are even quieter than uilleann pipes and have a very distinct, mellow sound.


Irish Whistle (low D)

The irish whistle is easy for an uilleann piper to learn as it has very similar fingerings to the pipes. The grip is quite a bit longer which took me some time to get used to. This instrument doesn't need much explanation, since it's basically a flute without the embouchure.


Accordion (B/C Diatonic Button)

I picked up this instrument in college, hoping to play in some Irish sessions. Three years later, after substantial practice I am still at a beginner level. The hardest part about this instrument is learning the fingering, and it's bloody hard. Because every button plays two notes depending on whether you are pushing or pulling. Probably the hardest instrument I've ever tried to learn.


Ukulele

I started playing the ukulele in high school after hearing "Somewhere over the Rainbow" by Israel Kamakawiwoʻole and the cover of "Can't Stop Falling in Love" by 21 Pilots. It's good fun to just play around with and sing a few songs.
