Why does DuoLingo Suck?

DuoLingo has become one of if not the most popular language learning app on the market today. I recently saw an ad for it that said something along the lines of "scientifically proven best method for learning a language," and I have never heard a bigger pile of bullshit.

There are a few reasons DuoLingo sucks.

1. NO RECALL. The most critical part of learning or memorizing anything is recall. When you are forced to remember something locked away in your mind, it becomes more automatic the next time you need to remember it. This is basically the only way to learn anything. THERE IS NOT A SINGLE GAME IN DUOLINGO THAT FORCES RECALL. There are a number of games you play. 1. Multiple choice 2. Repeat back 3. Fill in the blank (with multiple choice) 4. Construct a sentence (with given words) 5. Some others? None of these require any serious thought! You can do them mindlessly without learning anything. DuoLingo is a GAME, not a language learning app, and it's for people who want to feel like their learning something without actually learning anything.

2. Almost entirely auditory. Humans are not great at learning things by ear. Besides a very small minority of individuals, most people learn best in this order. 1. Auditory 2. Visual 3. Kinesthetic Most people learn best "kinesthetically", which is to say, by actually doing it. This is quite difficult to do in a language app, but the next best thing is visual learning, WHICH DUOLINGO FAILS MISERABLY AT. The only time visuals are used is in some of the multiple choice games to learn new vocabulary, and that is one of the least common games.

3. Everything is translated. It is far more difficult to learn a language when you translate everything into your own language. When you do this, you are essentially adding a layer of complexity to learning the new language, like you are forcing everything through filter. It works far better to learn the language DIRECTLY, by interacting with the real world and developing your lingustic skill adjacently. That's how we all learned our first language, duh! This is something almost all language apps miss out on. Rosetta Stone does this a little bit because at least they actually show you pictures to go along with what it's saying. I hope I've convinced you to not use duolingo anymore. At least you know the principles that define effective language learning. For tips on how to actually learn a language, check out my videos.
